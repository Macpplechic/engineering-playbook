# Communication Expectations

Communication isn't talking at people. It's talking with people. There's a difference, and I take it seriously.

## What I expect from you

**Be direct.** Say what you mean. Don't soften things to make them easier to hear — that helps no one. If something is broken, say it's broken. If you disagree, say you disagree. I can handle it. I'd rather have an uncomfortable truth than a comfortable silence.

**Say it once, clearly.** Don't bury the lead in four paragraphs of context. Lead with the point, then give the context. Async or in person — same rule.

**Close the loop.** If I hand you something or we agree on an action, I expect to hear back when it's done — or earlier if something changes. Don't make me chase you.

**Tell me early when things go sideways.** I have no interest in surprises. Bad news early is manageable. Bad news late is a crisis. Come to me with the problem and what you're thinking about it.

## What you can expect from me

I will be direct with you. I will give you feedback in real time — not saved up for a review cycle. I will adapt my communication style to what works best for you, but I won't change what I'm saying to make it more comfortable.

I will listen. I will ask why. I will change my mind when you give me a good reason to.

I will not micromanage your time. We are all adults. But I will hold you to what you commit to.

## On meetings

If a meeting doesn't need to be on the calendar, let's take it off. I have no attachment to recurring meetings that have outlived their purpose. If we can handle it async, we should.

When we do meet — be present. Come prepared. Know what you need from the conversation.
